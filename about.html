About Me

I graduated from the University of Oklahoma with a dual-degree with a B. S. in Meteorology and B. A. in Mathematics. For my Master's work, I remained at the University of Oklahoma at the Advanced Radar Research Center under the advisement of Dr. David Bodine and Dr. Anthony Reinhart. The focus of my research was investigating the impact of surface terrain on tornado dynamics through large-eddy vortex simulations. For my doctoral research, I conducted a detailed case study on the 17 May 2019 tornadic supercell observed during TORUS through multi-Doppler wind syntheses and thermodynamic retrievals under the advisement of Dr. Michael Coniglio and Dr. Erik Rasmussen. Both studies are now first-authored published work which you can find here and here. I also completed my postdoctoral research at the Cooperative Institute for Severe and High-Impact Weather Research and Operations (CIWRO), conducting a longitudinal analysis of supercell updrafts using multi-Doppler analyses.

See My Past Research


Starting in September of 2025, I joined Rainmaker Technology Corporation as a Meteorological Radar Scientist, working on the research team to design and optimize radar-based QPE products, as well as process, analyze, and extract insights from radar and other meteorological data to improve cloud seeding strategies. Our end goal within Rainmaker is to support sustainable water resource management through responsible weather modification.
