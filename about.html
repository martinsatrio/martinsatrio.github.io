About Me

I graduated from the University of Oklahoma with a dual-degree with a B. S. in Meteorology and B. A. in Mathematics. For my Master's work, I remained at the University of Oklahoma at the Advanced Radar Research Center under the advisement of Dr. David Bodine and Dr. Anthony Reinhart. The focus of my research was investigating the impact of surface terrain on tornado dynamics through large-eddy vortex simulations.

For my PhD research, I conducted a detailed case study on the 17 May 2019 tornadic supercell observed during TORUS through multi-Doppler wind syntheses and thermodynamic retrievals.

Currently, I am working as a post-doctoral research associate at the Cooperative Institute for Severe and High-Impact Weather Research and Operations. My current research involves establishing a longitudinal analysis of updraft and forward-flank characteristics of supercells using both ground-based and airborne radar data from the multi-year TORUS field campaign.

Outside of work, I enjoy watching college football, traveling, trying new foods, and spending time with my wife and two dogs!
